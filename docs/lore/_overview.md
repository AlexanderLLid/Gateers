# World Overview

Entry point to the Gaters lore. Short by design — link out (src: raw/design-overview.md).

## Premise
A long-dormant, galaxy-spanning network of ancient **Gates** suddenly switches on (see [[The Wake]]). Each player controls a single **planet-base** sitting on one Gate. The [[Central Authority]] deputizes independent operators to find, claim, and hold Gate-worlds — a gold rush over **finite, un-manufacturable Gate infrastructure**. Reach and wealth require opening your Gate; opening your Gate is the only way you can be attacked. [decided]

## Design thesis — "reach equals exposure"
You act on the world only as far as you've opened to it, you choose when and toward whom, and you cannot be touched while absent. "MAD in space." This is the lore framing of the [[Systems Overview|systems]]. [decided]

## The Gates & the Builders
Gates **woke sealed** and **cannot be manufactured** — only found, claimed, repaired. Who built them, and why, is **unknown to everyone by design** — see [[the-builders|The Builders]], the long-game mystery engine. Do not invent Builder lore. How gating works (bound to real physics) is in [[gate-physics|Gate Physics]]; the object itself is [[Gates]]. [decided]

## The mystery
The authority's "we flipped a dormant relay" is a cover story; something *else* woke the network and is using it too. Candidate truths are tracked in open-questions.md. [tentative]

## Themes & tone
Frontier sci-fi gold-rush among the bones of a vanished precursor civilization, with an undercurrent of cosmic unease. Exact register open. [tentative]
